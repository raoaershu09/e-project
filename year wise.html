YEAR WISE LISTING

  • 1933
    Influenza Virus
    Influenza viruses cause seasonal flu outbreaks, leading to respiratory illnesses ranging from mild to severe. Different strains of the virus emerge over time, necessitating new vaccine each year!
  • 1943
    Dengue Virus
    Dengue virus is transmitted by mosquitoes and causes dengue fever, a flu-like illness. Severe cases can lead to dengue hemorrhagic fever, which can be life-threatening.
  • 1947
    Zika Virus
    Zika virus is transmitted primarily through mosquito bites. It gained attention due to its association with birth defects, particularly microcephaly, in babies born to infected mothers.
  • 1956
    Human Papillomavirus(HPV)
    HPV is a group of viruses that can lead to gential warts and cancers, including cervical, anal, and oropharyngeal cancers.
  • 1976
    Ebola Virus
    The Ebola virus causes Ebola virus disease(EVD), a severe and often fatal illness in humans. It is characterized by fever, fatigue, muscle pain, and internal bleeding.
  • 1989
    Hepatitis C Virus(HCV)
    Hepatitis C virus primarily targets the liver, causing inflammation and potentially leading to chronic liver disease. It is transmitted through blood-to-blood contact.
  • 2019
    Severe Acute Respiratory Syndrome Coronavirus 2(SARS-CoV-2)
    SARS-CoV-2 is responsible for the COVID-19 pandemic.It is a novel coronavirus that primarily spreads through respiratory droplets.COVID-19 symptoms range from mild respiratory issues to severe pneumonia, and in some cases, it can lead to acute respiratory distress syndrome(ARDS)and death. The virus was first identified in Wuhan, China, in December 2019. It has since spread globally, resulting in widespread illness, healthcare challenges, and social economic disruptions. Efforts to control the pandemic have included public health measures, and medical research.
